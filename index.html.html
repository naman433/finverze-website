The problem

Schools teach formulas.
Nobody teaches money.

Your students will manage salaries, loans, investments, and taxes within 5 years of leaving your school. None of that is in the curriculum.

01
12 years of school. Zero finance classes.
Trigonometry, thermodynamics, the French Revolution — all covered. How a bank account actually grows your money? Never mentioned.
02
First salary arrives. Nobody prepared them.
Students graduate, start earning, and immediately repeat the financial patterns of adults who also never learned — spend first, save whatever's left, which is usually nothing.
03
A one-day talk doesn't change behaviour.
Awareness is not enough. A student who attends a 60-minute workshop forgets 70% of it within 48 hours. Real change requires structure, repetition, and accountability over time.
04

After PUC, students pick courses, take education loans, and start spending independently. The habits formed now — good or bad — follow them for decades. This is the right moment.

The Finverze Cohort

Not a course.
A structured experience.

7 days. Every session builds on the last. Delivered by a certified CA using Finverze's curriculum — built specifically for PUC students.

1
See Your Money
Personal cash flow audit. Students map every rupee in and out — many for the first time. CA leads the session live.
2
Needs, Wants & The Money Drain
Sort every spend. Learn the 50/30/20 rule adapted for student life. Calculate where your money actually goes.
3
The Power of Not Spending
The 24-hour pause rule. Impulse mapping. The no-spend challenge — the hardest day, and the most transformative.
4
Your First Savings Goal
Goal-based saving vs saving "whatever's left." Students set a specific target, calculate the monthly amount, and sign a commitment.
5
How Money Grows
Compound interest with real numbers. Savings account vs mutual fund SIP. Students calculate their own 20-year scenarios.
6
Money Myths & The Real World
Five myths busted by a CA with evidence. Investing, credit cards, savings accounts — all demystified with facts.
7
Your Personal Money Blueprint
Students build a one-page signed financial plan. Certificate ceremony. The document they take home and keep.
